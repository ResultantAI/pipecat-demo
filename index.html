Worker Type Classification

Refers to the employment relationship structure between employer and worker.

W2 Employee: Traditional employment with taxes withheld by employer, benefits eligibility, set schedule, company equipment provided. Subject to company policies and direct supervision.

1099 Contractor: Independent contractor manages own taxes (quarterly estimated payments), no benefits, flexible arrangement, provides own tools/workspace. More autonomy but less job security.

Example: A full-time software engineer with health benefits and 401(k) is W2. A freelance designer hired for a 3-month website project is 1099.
